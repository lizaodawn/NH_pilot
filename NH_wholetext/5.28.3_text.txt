on passing telmessus we come to the asiatic or carpathian sea, and the district which is properly called asia. agrippa has divided this region into two parts; one of which he has bounded on the east by phrygia and lycaonia, on the west by the aegean sea, on the south by the egyptian sea, and on the north by paphlagonia, making its length to be 473 miles and its breadth 320. the other part he has bounded by the lesser armenia on the east, phrygia, lycaonia, and pamphylia on the west, the province of pontus on the north, and the sea of pamphylia on the south, making it 575 miles in length and 325 in breadth.
