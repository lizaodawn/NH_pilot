helenium, which had its origin, as i have said, in the tears of helen, is believed to preserve physical charm, and to keep unimpaired the fresh complexion of our women, whether of the face or of the rest of the body. moreover, it is supposed that by its use they gain a kind of attractiveness and sex-appeal. to this plant when taken in wine is attributed the power of stimulating gaiety, the power possessed by the famous nepenthes extolled by homer of banishing all sorrow. it also has a very sweet juice. the root of it, taken in water fasting, is good for asthma; inside it is white and sweet. it is also taken in wine for snake bites. pounded it is said further to kill mice.
