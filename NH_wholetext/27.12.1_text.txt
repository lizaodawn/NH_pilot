anonis, which some prefer to call ononis, is branchy, and like fenugreek, except that it is more bushy and more hairy. it has an agreeable smell, and becomes prickly after spring. preserved in brine it is also used as food, while the fresh plant cauterizes the edges of ulcers. the root is boiled down in vinegar and water for toothache, and taken in drink with honey it also expels stone from the bladder. for epilepsy it is given in oxymel boiled down to one half.
