aparine, called by some omphalocarpos, by others philanthropos, is branchy, hairy, and with five or six leaves arranged at intervals in a circle around the branches. the seed is round, hard, hollowed, and rather sweet. it grows in cornfields, or gardens, or meadows, and is so prickly as even to cling to the clothes. the seed, taken in drachma doses in wine, is efficacious against the bite of serpents and poisonous spiders. the leaves, applied locally, check excessive bleeding from wounds. the juice is poured into the ears.
