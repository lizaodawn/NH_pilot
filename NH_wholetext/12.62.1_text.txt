there is also another tree that likewise serves for producing unguents, which is called by some people an elate — the latin for which is 'fir' — and by others a palm and by others again a spatula. that of hammonium is most highly spoken of, next the egyptian variety, and then the scythian. it only has a scent if it grows in regions devoid of water; it has tears of a greasy consistency, which are added to unguents to overcome the hardness of the oil.
