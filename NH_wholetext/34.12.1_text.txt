i also find that statues were erected to pythagoras and to alcibiades, in the corners of the place of assembly, when during one of our samnite wars pythian apollo had commanded the erection in some conspicuous position of an effigy of the bravest man of the greek race, and likewise, one of the wisest man; these remained until sulla the dictator made the senate-house on the site. it is surprising that those illustrious senators of ours rated pythagoras above socrates, whom the same deity had put above all the rest of mankind in respect of wisdom, or rated alcibiades above so many other men in manly virtue, or anybody above themistocles for wisdom and manly virtue combined.
