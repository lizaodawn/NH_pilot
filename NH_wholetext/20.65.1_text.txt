cultivated cunila too has its uses. the juice with rose oil is good for the ear-laps, and it is taken by itself in drink for stings. from it grows the mountain variety, which is like wild thyme and efficacious against the bites of serpents. it is diuretic and cleanses after childbirth. wild or cultivated it is a wonderful stimulus to digestion and to the appetite, or relieves indigestion taken fasting and sprinkled in a drink. useful too for sprains, taken in barley meal with vinegar and water it is very useful for the stings of wasps and the like. other kinds of libanotis will be dealt with in their proper place.
