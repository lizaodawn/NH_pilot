some writers have stated that the distance between the euxine and the caspian sea is not more than three hundred and seventy-five miles; cornelius nepos makes it only two hundred and fifty. within such straits is asia pent up in this second instance by the agency of the sea! claudius caesar has informed us that from the cimmerian bosporus to the caspian sea is a distance of only one hundred and fifty miles, and that nicator seleucus contemplated cutting through this isthmus just at the time when he was slain by ptolemy ceraunus. it is a well-known fact that the distance from the gates of caucasus to the shores of the euxine is two hundred miles.
