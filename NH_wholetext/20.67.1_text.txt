origanum, which rivals cunila in its wild flavour, as i have said has many varieties useful in medicine. one is onitis, called by some prasion, and not unlike hyssop. its special use is to be taken in warm water for gnawings of the stomach and indigestion, and in white wine for the stings of spiders and scorpions, while it is applied on wool with vinegar and oil for sprains and bruises.
