the seed of lychnis too, that flame-coloured flower, is crushed and taken in wine for snake bites and for the stings of scorpions, hornets and the like. the wild variety of this plant is injurious to the stomach. it loosens the bowels, in doses of two drachmae, bringing away bile most effectively, and is so hurtful to scorpions that the mere sight of it sends them into complete stupor. its root is called bolites by the people of asia; tied over the eye it is said to remove white film on the pupil.
