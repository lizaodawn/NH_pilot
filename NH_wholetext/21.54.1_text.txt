some plants then are prickly, while others are without prickles. of prickly plants the species are many. of nothing but prickle are asparagus and scorpio, for they have no leaves at all. some prickly plants, however, have leaves, for instance thistle, erynge, glycyrrhiza and nettle. for all these have a sharp sting in their leaves. some have foliage also along the prickly spine, as caltrop and rest-harrow. some again have prickles not on the leaves but on the stem, as pheos, that some have called stoebe. hippopheos has prickly joints. a peculiar characteristic of the caltrop is that it has also a prickly fruit.
