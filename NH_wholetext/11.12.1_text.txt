they build large and splendid separate palaces for those who are to be their rulers, in the bottom of the hive; these project with a protuberance, and if this be squeezed out, no offspring is born. all the cells are hexagonal, each side being made by one of the bee's six feet. none of these tasks are done at a fixed time, but they snatch their duties on fine days. they fill their cells with honey on one or at most two days.
