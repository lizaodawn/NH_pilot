glaux, called of old eugalacton, has leaves like those of cytisus and the lentil; they are whiter underneath. the branches, five or six in number, extremely slender and springing from the root, lie along the ground; on them form small, purple blossoms. it is found near the sea, and is boiled in similago porridge to stimulate a rich supply of milk; those who have drunk a dose should proceed to a bath.
