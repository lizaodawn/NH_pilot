other instances of the fickleness of mortal fortunes are these: homer has recorded that men of such diverse fates as hector and polydamas were born on the same night; marcus caelius rufus and gaius licinius calvus, both orators but with such different success, were born on the same day, may 28 in the consulship [82 bc] of gaius marius and gnaeus carbo — the latter's third. taking the entire world, this happens daily even to persons born at the same hours — masters and slaves, kings and paupers come into existence simultaneously.
