danaus first came from egypt to greece by ship; before that time rafts were used for navigation, having been invented by king erythras for use between the islands in the red sea. persons are found who think that vessels were devised earlier on the hellespont by the mysians and trojans when they crossed to war against the thracians. even now in the british ocean coracles are made of wicker with hide sown round it, and on the nile canoes are made of papyrus, rushes and reeds. the first voyage made in a long ship is attributed by philostephanus to jason, by hegesias to parhalus, by ctesias to semiramis, and by archemachus to aegaeon. further advances were as follows:
