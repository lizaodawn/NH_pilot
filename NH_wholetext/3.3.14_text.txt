(2.) at the present day the length of baetica, from the town of castulo, on its frontier, to gades is 250 miles, and from murgi, which lies on the sea-coast, twenty-five miles more. the breadth, measured from the coast of carteia, is 234 miles. who is there that can entertain the belief that agrippa, a man of such extraordinary diligence, and one who bestowed so much care on his subject, when he proposed to place before the eyes of the world a survey of that world, could be guilty of such a mistake as this, and that too when seconded by the late emperor the divine augustus? for it was that emperor who completed the [vipsania] portico which had been begun by his sister, and in which the survey was to be kept, in conformity with the plan and descriptions of m. agrippa.
