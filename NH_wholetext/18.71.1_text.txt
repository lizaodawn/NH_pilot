the following arc the rural operations belonging to this interval: to turn up the ground again, to dig round the trees, or to bank them up where a hot locality calls for it — except in a very rich soil crops just budding must not be dug — to clean seed-plots with the hoe, to harvest barley, to prepare the threshing-floor for the harvest, in cato's opinion by dressing it with olive-lees, and in virgil's with chalk, a more laborious method. but for the most part people only level it and smear it with a rather weak solution of cow-dung; this appears to be enough to prevent dust.
