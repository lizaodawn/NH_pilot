the leaves of the holly, crushed and with the addition of salt, are good for diseases of the joints, while the berries are good for menstruation, coeliac trouble, dysentery and cholera. taken in wine they check looseness of the bowels. an application of the decocted root extracts objects embedded in the flesh, and is very useful for dislocations and swellings. a holly tree planted in a town house or country house keeps off magic influences. pythagoras has recorded that by its blossom water is solidified, and that a holly stick, cast at any animal, even if through want of strength in the thrower it falls short of the quarry, of its own accord rolls nearer the mark, so powerful is the nature of this tree. the smoke of the yew tree kills rats and mice.
