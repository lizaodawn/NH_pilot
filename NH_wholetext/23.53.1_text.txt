the palm called date or spathe gives to medicine its buds, leaves, and bark. its leaves are applied to the hypochondria, stomach, liver, and to sores that spread and refuse to form a scar. the tender bark of it, mixed with resin and wax, heals the itch in twenty days. a decoction of it also is used for diseases of the testicles. it darkens the hair, and fumigation with it brings away the foetus. it is given in drink for diseases of the kidneys, bladder and hypochondria, though it is injurious to the head and sinews. a decoction of it arrests fluxes of the uterus and of the belly; the ashes also cure colic, and taken in white wine are very beneficial for affections of the uterus.
