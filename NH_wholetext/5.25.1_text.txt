lycaonia. these people are bounded by lycaonia, which belongs to the jurisdiction of the province of asia, to which also resort the people of philomelium, tymbrium, leucolithium, pelta, and tyrium. to this jurisdiction is also added a tetrarchy of lycaonia in that part which joins up to galatia, containing fourteen states, with the famous city of iconium. in lycaonia itself the most noted places are thebasa on taurus, and hyde, on the confines of galatia and cappadocia. on the [western] side of lycaonia, and above pamphylia, come the milyae, a people descended from the thracians; their city is arycanda.
