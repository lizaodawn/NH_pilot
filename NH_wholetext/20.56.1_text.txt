there is likewise kinship between pennyroyal and catmint. boiled down to one third in water they disperse chills, help menstruation and allay the heats of summer. catmint also has power to counteract the poisons of serpents. the smoke and smell of burning catmint drives them away; so those about to sleep in fear of snakes had better place catmint under the bedclothes. the pounded plant is applied to lachrymal fistula, and the fresh plant with one third part of bread mixed in vinegar is used as a liniment for headache. the juice of it dropped into the nostrils when thrown back stops bleeding at the nose; the root likewise, which with myrtle seed makes in warm raisin-wine a gargle that heals quinsy.
