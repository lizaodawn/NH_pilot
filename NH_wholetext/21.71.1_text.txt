the root of the rush in three heminae of water, boiled down to one third, is a cure for coughs. the seed roasted and taken in water checks diarrhoea and excessive menstruation. the rush, however, called holoschoenus brings on headaches. the nearest parts to the root are chewed as a remedy for the bites of spiders. i find that there is also one other kind of rush, called euripice. its seed is said to induce sleep, but the dose must be kept small, or coma will result.
