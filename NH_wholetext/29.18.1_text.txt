asps kill those they strike by torpor and coma, inflicting of all serpents the most incurable bites. but their venom, if it comes into contact with the blood or a fresh wound, is immediately fatal, if with an old sore, its action is delayed. apart from this, however much is drunk, it is harmless, having no corrosive property. and so the flesh of animals killed by their bite may be eaten with safety. i should hesitate to put forward a remedy obtained from these creatures, had not marcus varro, in the seventy-third year of his life, recorded that a sovereign remedy for asp bites is for the victim to drink his own urine.
