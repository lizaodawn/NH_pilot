blite seems to be an inactive plant, without flavour or any sharp quality, for which reason in menander husbands use the name as a term of abuse for their wives. it is injurious to the stomach. it so disturbs the bowels as to cause in some persons. it is said however to be good for scorpion stings when drunk in wine, for corns on the feet when applied in a liniment, and also, with oil, for diseases of the spleen and for pain in the temples. used as a food it is thought by hippocrates to check menstruation.
