tripolion is found on coastal rocks washed by the waves, but neither in the sea nor on dry ground; the leaf is that of isatis only thicker, the stem a span high and divided at the end, and the root white, thick, with a strong smell and a hot taste. cooked in emmer wheat it is prescribed for patients with liver complaint. this plant is thought by some to be the same as polium, about which i have spoken in its proper place.
