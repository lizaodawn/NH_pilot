the chief kind of lettuce growing wild is the one called goat-lettuce, which when thrown into the sea kills immediately all the fish in the neighbourhood. its milk, or juice, when thickened and then added to vinegar, in doses of two oboli to one cyathus of water, is prescribed for dropsical patients. the crushed stalk and leaves, sprinkled with salt, cure a cut sinew. the pounded plant and vinegar, used as a mouthwash twice a month in the morning, keeps away toothache.
