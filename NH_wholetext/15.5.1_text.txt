olive-oil has the property of imparting warmth to the body and protecting it against cold, and also that of cooling the head when heated. those parents of all the vices, the greeks, have diverted the use of olive-oil to serve the purpose of luxury by making it a regular practice in their gymnasiums; the governors of those institutions a have been known to sell the scrapings of the oil for 80,000 sesterces. the majesty of rome has bestowed great honour on the olive-tree by decorating our cavalry squadrons with wreaths of olive on july, and also when they are celebrating a minor triumph. athens also crowns victorious athletes with olive wreaths, and greece the victors at olympia with wreaths of wild olive.
