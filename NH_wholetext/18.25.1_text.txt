the provinces of gaul, and particularly aquitaine, also use panic, and so also do the parts of italy on the banks of the po, though adding to it beans without water. the races of the black sea prefer panic to any other food. all the other kinds of summer corn flourish even better in land watered by streams than in rainy districts, but millet and panic are not at all fond of water, as it makes them to leaves. people advise not growing them among vines or fruit trees, as they believe that this crop impoverishes the soil.
