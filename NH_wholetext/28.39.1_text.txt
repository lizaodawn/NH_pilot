the various marrows too are all in use. all marrow is emollient, filling, drying, and warming. the most highly valued is that of deer, next of calves, and then of goats, male and female. marrow is prepared before autumn; it should be fresh, washed, dried in the shade, then passed melted through a sieve, strained through a linen cloth, and then stored away in an earthenware vessel in a cool place.
