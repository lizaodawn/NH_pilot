some trees producing fruit bear it both of on the sides and at the end of their branches, for instance the pear, the pomegranate, the fig and the myrtle. in other respects they have the same nature as cereal plants, for in their case also the ear grows at the tip of the stalks, whereas beans grow on the sides. the palm-tree alone, as has been stated, has its fruit, enclosed in spathes, hanging down in bunches.
