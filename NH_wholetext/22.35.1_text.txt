bupleuron is considered by the greeks to be among the vegetables growing wild. it has a stem a cubit high, many and long leaves, and the head of dill. hippocrates recommends it as a food, glauco and nicander as a medicine. its seed counteracts the poison of serpents. the leaves or the juice they apply in wine for the removal of the afterbirth, and the leaves with salt and wine for scrofulous swellings. its root is given in wine for snake bites and as a diuretic.
