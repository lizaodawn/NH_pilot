for the present i forbear to speak of its genius, its manners, its men, and the nations whom it has conquered by eloquence and force of arms. the very greeks themselves, a race fond in the extreme of expatiating on their own praises, have amply given judgment in its favour, when they named but a small part of it 'magna graecia.' but we must be content to do on this occasion as we have done in our description of the heavens; we must only touch upon some of these points, and take notice of but a few of its stars. i only beg my readers to bear in mind that i am thus hastening on for the purpose of giving a general description of everything that is known to exist throughout the whole earth.
