the waters in the island of aenaria are said to cure stone in the bladder, as does also the water called acidula — it is a cold one — four miles from teanum sidicinum, that at stabiae called dimidia, and the water of venafrum from the spring acidulus. the same result comes from drinking the water of lake velia, also of the syrian spring near mount taurus, according to marcus varro, and of the phrygian river gallus, according to callimachus. but here moderation is necessary in drinking lest it drive people to madness, which ctesias writes those suffer from who drink of the red spring in ethiopia.
