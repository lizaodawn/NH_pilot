lycia formerly contained seventy towns, now it has but thirty-six. of these, the most celebrated, besides those already mentioned, are canas, candyba, so celebrated for the oenian/eunian grove, podalia, choma, past which the river aedesa flows, cyaneae, ascandia, [lisa? ], amelas, noscopium, tlos, and telandrus. it includes also in the interior the district of cabalia, the three cities of which are oenianda, balbura, and bubon.
