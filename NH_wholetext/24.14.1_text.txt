similar is the nature of ammoniacum and of its tear, which should be tested in the way i mentioned. it softens, warms, disperses, and dissolves. in eye salves it promotes clearness of vision. it removes itch, scars, and white spots on the eyes, and relieves toothache, more effectively when it has been set alight. it is good for difficulty of breathing, pleurisy, affections of the lungs and bladder, blood in the urine, diseases of the spleen, and sciatica, if it be taken in drink — thus administered it also loosens the bowels — and, boiled with an equal weight of pitch or wax and with rose oil, it makes a good ointment for diseases of the joints and for gouty pains. it brings superficial abscesses to a head, and extracts corns, when mixed with honey — so applied it also softens indurations — and combined with vinegar and cyprian wax or rose oil it makes a very effective application for diseases of the spleen. a rubbing with ointment made up of this gum, with vinegar, oil and a little soda, is a good remedy for fatigue.
