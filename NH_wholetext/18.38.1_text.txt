the fitch also is not difficult to cultivate. this needs weeding more than the vetch; and it too has medicinal properties, indeed the fact that his late majesty augustus was cured by it stands on record in his own letters. five pecks of seed are enough for one yoke of oxen in a day. it is said to be injurious to oxen if sown in march and to cause cold in the head if sown in autumn, but sowing it in early spring makes it harmless.
