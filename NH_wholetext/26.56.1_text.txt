silaus grows in running streams with gravelly bottoms; a cubit high it resembles celery. it is boiled as is an acid vegetable, and is very good for the bladder, which if it suffers from is cured by the root of panaces, a plant otherwise injurious to the bladder. stone is expelled by the wild apple, a pound of the root being boiled down to one half in a congius of wine — a hemina of it is taken daily for three days, the rest is taken in wine — by sea-nettle, by daucum, and by the seed of plantain in wine.
