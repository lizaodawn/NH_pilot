there is another hypericon, called by some caro, having a leaf like that of the tamarisk — it grows underneath it — but more fleshy and less red. it is scented, more than a span high, with a sweet and rather pungent taste. the seed is of a heating nature and therefore causes inflammation, but it is not injurious to the stomach; it is particularly good for strangury, if the bladder is not ulcerated. taken in wine it is also good for pleurisy,
