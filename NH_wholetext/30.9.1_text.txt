they assert that the taste in the mouth is made agreeable if the teeth are rubbed with the ash of burnt mice mixed with honey; some add fennel root. if the teeth are picked with a vulture's feather, they make the breath sour. to pick them with a porcupine's quill conduces to their firmness. sores on the tongue or lips are healed by a decoction of swallows in honey wine; chaps on them by goose grease or hen's grease, by oesypum with gall nut, by white webs of spiders, or by the small webs spun on rafters. if the mouth has been scalded by over-hot things, bitch's milk will give an immediate cure.
