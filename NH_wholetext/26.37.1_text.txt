of polypodium, a plant called by romans filicula, being like a fern (filix), the root is medicinal, hairy, grass-green inside, as thick as the little finger, with indented edges so as to look like a polypus's arms, of a sweetish taste, and to be found in stony soils or under old trees. the juice is extracted from the root soaked in water, and chopped up fine the root itself is sprinkled on cabbage, beet, mallows and pickled fish, or else boiled with gruel to make a gentle aperient usable even in fever. it brings away bile and phlegm, although injurious to the stomach. dried and reduced to powder it eats away polypus if pushed up the nostrils. there is no flower and no seed.
