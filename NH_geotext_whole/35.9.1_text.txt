but it was the dictator caesar who gave outstanding public importance to pictures by dedicating paintings of ajax and medea in front of the temple of venus genetrix; and after him marcus agrippa, a man who stood nearer to rustic simplicity than to refinements. at all events there is preserved a speech of agrippa, lofty in tone and worthy of the greatest of the citizens, on the question of making all pictures and statues national property, a procedure which would have been preferable to banishing them to country houses. however, that same severe spirit paid the city of cyzicus 1,200,000 sesterces for two pictures, an ajax and an aphrodite; he had also had small paintings let into the marble even in the warmest part of his hot baths; which were removed a short time ago when the baths were being repaired.
