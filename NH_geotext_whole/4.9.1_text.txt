argolis: the next gulf, which extends as far as scyllaion, is called the argolic gulf, being fifty miles across, and 162 in circuit. the towns upon it are, boea, epidaurus surnamed limera, zarax, and the port of cyphanta. the rivers are the inachus and the erasinus, between which lies argos, surnamed hippium, situate beyond the place called lerna, and at a distance of two miles from the sea. nine miles farther is mycenae, and the place where, it is said, tiryns stood; the site, too, of mantinea. the mountains are, artemius, apesantus, asterion, parparus, and some others, eleven in number. the fountains are those of niobe, amymone, and psamathe.
