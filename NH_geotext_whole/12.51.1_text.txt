a tree found in egypt is the cypros, which has the leaves of the jujube-tree and the white, scented seed of the coriander. cypros-seed is boiled in olive oil and afterwards crushed, producing the cypros of commerce, which sells at 5 denarii a pound. the best is made from the tree grown at canopus on the banks of the nile, the second best at ascalon in judea, and the third quality on the island of cyprus, which has a sort of sweet scent. the cypros is said to be the same as the thorn called privet in italy.
