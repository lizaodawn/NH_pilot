authorities say that there is a plane-tree at delphi that was planted by the hand of agamemnon, and also another at caphya, a place in arcadia. there are trees at the present day growing on the tomb of protesilaus on the shore of the dardanelles opposite the city of the trojans, which in every period since the time of protesilaus, after they have grown big enough to command a view of ilium, wither away and then revive again; while the oaks on the tomb of ilus near the city are said to have been planted at the date when the place first began to be called ilium.
