there are also two kinds that are known only to the avaricious herd, as they are very profitable articles of trade. first comes madder, which is indispensable for dyeing woollens and leather; the most highly esteemed is the italian, and especially that grown in the neighbourhood of rome, and almost all the provinces teem with it. it grows of itself, but a variety that resembles fitch, but has prickly leaves and stalk, is also grown from seed. this plant has a jointed stem, with five leaves arranged in a circle round each joint. the seed is red and finally turns black, and the root red. its medicinal properties we shall state in their proper place.
