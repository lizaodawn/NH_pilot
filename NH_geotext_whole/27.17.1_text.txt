asplenon, called by some hemionion, has many leaves four inches long, a slimy root, pitted as is a fern's, whitish and hairy. there is no stem, flower or seed. it grows on rocks and on shaded, damp walls, the most approved kind in crete. a decoction of its leaves in vinegar, taken as a draught for thirty days, is said to reduce the spleen, the leaves being also applied locally. they relieve too hiccoughs. this plant, as it causes barrenness, must not be given to women.
