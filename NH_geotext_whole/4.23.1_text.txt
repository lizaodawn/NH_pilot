the sporades: the islands thus far are considered as belonging to the cyclades; the rest that follow are the sporades. these are, helene, phacussa, nicasia, schinussa, pholegandros, and, at a distance of thirty-eight miles from naxos, icaros, which has given its name to the surrounding sea, and is the same number of miles in length, with two cities, and a third now no longer in existence: this island used formerly to be called doliche, macris, and ichthyoessa. it is situate fifty miles to the north-east of delos, and thirty-five from the island of samos. between euboea and andros, there is an arm of the sea ten miles in width, and from icaros to geraistos is a distance of 112 1/2 miles.
