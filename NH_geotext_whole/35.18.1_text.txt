paraetonium is called after the place of that name in egypt. it is said to be sea-foam hardened with mud, and this is why tiny shells are found in it. it also occurs in the island of crete and in cyrene. at rome it is adulterated with cimolian clay which has been boiled and thickened. the price of the best quality is 50 denarii per 6 lbs. it is the most greasy of all the white colours and makes the most tenacious for plasters because of its smoothness.
