we have been told of strange kinds birds in the hercynian forest of germany whose feathers shine like fires at nighttime; but in the other forests nothing noteworthy occurs beyond the notoriety caused by remoteness. the most celebrated water-bird in parthian seleucia and in asia is the phalaris-duck, the most celebrated bird in colchis the pheasant - it droops and raises its two feathered ears - and in the numidian part of africa the numidic fowl all of these are now found in italy.
