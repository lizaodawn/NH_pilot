in syria very great pains are taken over kitchen-gardens; hence the greek proverb: 'syrians have plenty of vegetables.' they sow a vegetable called by some gingidion that is very like staphylinus, only it is slighter and more bitter, though its properties are the same. lit is eaten, cooked or raw, with great advantage to the stomach, for it dries up all its humours, however deep these may lie.
