incidentally i will also mention medicines obtained from the scented rush, for one place where such a rush grows is in coele syria, as i have related in the appropriate place. the most esteemed, however, comes from nabataea, known also as teuchitis; the next best is the babylonian, and the worst comes from africa, being without any scent. it is round, affecting the tongue with the stinging taste of sour wine. the genuine kind, on being rubbed, gives out a smell of roses, and the broken bits are red. dispersing flatulence, it is good for the stomach, and for those who vomit bile. it allays hiccoughs, promotes belching, is diuretic, and a remedy for bladder troubles. for female complaints a decoction is made. with dry resin it is applied to sufferers from opisthotonic tetanus because of its warming properties.
