messenia: further south is the gulf of cyparissus, with the city of cyparissa on its shores, the line of which is seventy-two miles in length. then, the towns of pylos and methone, the place where helos stood, the promontory of acritas, the asinaean gulf, which takes its name from the town of asine, and the coronean, so called from corone; which gulfs terminate at the promontory of taenarum. these are all in the country of messenia, which has eighteen mountains, and the river pamisus also. in the interior are messene, ithome, oichalia, arene, pteleon, thryon, dorion, and zancle, all of them known to fame at different periods. the margin of this gulf measures eighty miles, the distance across being thirty.
