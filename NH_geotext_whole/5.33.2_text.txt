eratosthenes tells us that in asia there have perished the nations of the solymi, the leleges, the bebryces, the colycantii, and the tripsedri. isidorus adds to these the arimi, as also the capretae, settled on the spot where apamea stands, which was founded by king seleucus, between cilicia, cappadocia, cataonia, and armenia, and was at first called damea, from the fact that it had conquered nations most remarkable for their fierceness.
