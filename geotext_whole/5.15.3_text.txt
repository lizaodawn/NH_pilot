asphaltites produces nothing whatever except bitumen, to which indeed it owes its name. the bodies of animals will not sink in its waters, and even those of bulls and camels float there. in length it exceeds 100 miles being at its greatest breadth twenty-five, and at its smallest six. arabia of the nomades faces it on the east, and machaerus on the south, at one time, next to hierosolyma, the most strongly fortified place in judaea. on the same side lies callirrhoe, a warm spring, remarkable for its medicinal qualities, and which, by its name, indicates the celebrity its waters have gained.
