it was a king of the illyrians named gentius who discovered gentian, which, though it grows everywhere, is most excellent when it grows in illyria. the leaf is like that of the ash but of the size of a lettuce leaf; the stem is tender and of the thickness of a thumb, hollow and empty, with leaves at intervals, sometimes three cubits in height, and growing from a pliant root, which is darkish and without smell. it grows abundantly a on watery slopes near the foot of the alps. the parts used are the root and the juice. the nature of the root is warming, but it should not be taken in drink by women with child.
