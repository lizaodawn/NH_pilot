the baetis does not rise, as some writers have asserted, near the town of mentesa, in the province of tarraco, but in the tugiensian forest; and near it rises the river tader, which waters the territory of carthage. at ilorcum it turns away from the funeral pile of scipio; then taking a sweep to the left, it falls into the atlantic ocean, giving its name to this province: at its source it is but small, though during its course it receives many other streams, which it deprives as well of their waters as their renown. it first enters baetica in ossigitania, and glides gently, with a smooth current, past many towns situate on either side of its banks.
