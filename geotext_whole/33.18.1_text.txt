at the present day we see ceilings covered with gold even in private houses, but they were first gilded in the capitol during the censorship of lucius mummius after the fall of carthage. [146 b.c.] from ceilings the use of gilding passed over also to vaulted roofs and walls, these too being now gilded like pieces of plate, whereas a variety of judgements were passed on catulus by his contemporaries for having gilded the brass tilings of the capitol.
